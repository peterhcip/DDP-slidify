Kepler's Third Law

Basics

Kepler's Third Law stipulates that the square of the orbital period of a planet is proportional to the cube of the semi-major axis of its orbit.

Simplification

As the orbits of most planets have only very small eccentricities, the semi-major axis can be approximated as the average distance from the Sun.

Simplification

If we express the distance from the Sun in Astronomical Units (AU) and the orbital period in years, then Kepler's Third Law can be simplified as Orbital Period = Distance ^ 1.5

Worked Example

As an example, the distance of Mars from the Sun is 1.5 AU. The orbital period of Mars is thus 1.5 ^ 1.5 = 1.84 years.

dist <- 1.5
OrbitPeriod <- dist^1.5
